About me

I'm a research fellow of Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) and a Postdoctoral researcher at Software Design and Analysis Laboratory (SDLAB), Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST), Japan. My research is focused on understanding and improving software development practices. By using statistical analysis, I aim to uncover empirical evidences and knowledge from data recorded in software repositories. In particular, my Ph.D. thesis aims to better understand the current practices of code review and to provide actionable insights and process support for practitioners to achieve high quality of software systems. I also currently holds JSPS KAKENHI grant to fund my research.
