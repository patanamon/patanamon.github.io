About me

I'm a postdoctoral fellow at the Software Analysis and Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL), Queen's University under the supervision of Professor Ahmed E. Hassan. My research is focused on understanding and improving software development practices. By using statistical analysis, I aim to uncover empirical evidences and knowledge from data recorded in software repositories. In particular, my Ph.D. thesis aims to better understand the current practices of code review and to provide actionable insights and process support for practitioners to achieve high quality of software systems.

I will join a faculty member as a lecturer at the School of Computer Science, the University of Adelaide (one of the top 125 QS World Universities Ranking and one of the Go8 Australian leading universities) on January 2018. I'm open to working with Master/Ph.D. students who enthusiastically want to learn how to mine big data and discover knowledge for our software engineering society!
