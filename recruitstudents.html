Fully-funded Ph.D. Scholarships in Data Science and Software Engineering at the University of Adelaide, Australia

PhD scholarships are now offered to those who have an interest in the broad areas of Data Science and Software Engineering. We are looking for highly-motivated and exceptional candidates who are interested in researching big data analytics in software engineering. The research will be performed under the supervision of Dr Patanamon Thongtanunam and her colleagues.

The scholarships provides:

  • Tuition fees for 3 years for a Doctoral research degree, and
  • An annual living stipend ($26,288 in 2017, tax exempt) for 3 years for a Doctoral research degree, plus conference travel awards.

Application Due:

30 April 2018 (Prospective applicants should contact at least 1 month prior to the university deadline.)

Minimum Requirements:

To be eligible for this scholarship, the applicant must meet the University of Adelaide’s entry requirements.
    1. The applicant shall be:
    1. a relevant Bachelor degree of the University of Adelaide that contains a research component deemed appropriate by the University and in which the candidate has achieved at least a IIA standard, or
    2. a relevant Master by Research degree of the University of Adelaide, or
    3. a relevant Master by Coursework degree of the University of Adelaide containing a minimum of 15 credit points by research, with an overall Grade Point Average (GPA) of 5.0 or higher and a GPA of 6.0 or higher in the Research Component.
  2. A person who holds a qualification of another university as specified in 1 above, or equivalent thereof, may be accepted as a candidate provided that the program of study undertaken and the academic standard reached are equivalent to those required of a candidate who is a graduate of the University of Adelaide.
  3. The University may accept as a candidate a graduate who does not qualify under Academic Program Rules 1 or 1 but who has demonstrated an outstanding level of academic achievement and is experienced in research as evidenced by significant research publications or written reports on research work done by the applicant.
  4. Applicants for a Doctor of Philosophy must satisfy the minimum English language proficiency requirement as set by the University:
    • Meet a TOEFL total score of 79 with 24 in Writing AND 22 in Speaking AND 13 in Listening and Reading.
    • Meet an IELTS overall band score of 6.5 AND minimum band scores 6.5 in Writing and Speaking AND 6.0 in Listening and Reading.

Application Package

To apply for one of these scholarships, you must complete an application consisting of:

  • An email title “[(Master/PhD) Scholarship Application] …your name …”
    • A cover letter outlining
    • the degree that a candidate would like to apply
    • your summary of accomplishment
    • why you want me to be your supervisor and how you think you fit into my research program
  • A one page research proposal outlining your interest and suitability for the project;
  • A detailed curriculum vitae;
  • Undergraduate andhonours/master transcripts;
  • If English is not your first language, please provide us with proof of English language proficiency (Only TOEFL and IELTS are eligible); and
  • Contact details for two academic referees.

Please submit your application via email directly to Dr Patanamon Thongtanunam (patanamon.thongtanunam[at]adelaide.edu.au).
